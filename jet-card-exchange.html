Benefits for Buyers

Liquidity without long lock‑ups

Buy verified hours when you need them—or resell unused hours through the Exchange where issuer terms allow. No multi‑year commitments.

Price certainty

Know your rate before you book. Our standardized term sheets make peak‑day surcharges, de‑icing, fuel/CPI escalators, and taxi time crystal clear.

Safety & service transparency

Every listing shows operator certificate (e.g., Part 135/91K), ARG/US or Wyvern status where applicable, cabin class, age bands, recovery policy and call‑out rules.

Escrow and dispute support

Funds sit in escrow until terms are met. If service differs from the listing, our resolution desk steps in and we make you whole per the policy.

AI contract scan

Upload your current card or fractional agreement. We extract key clauses—blackouts, interchange fees, transferability, expiration—and flag risks in minutes.

Group‑buy leverage

Join a Project Jet Buyers cohort. We aggregate hours and tender to multiple issuers to win capped rates, improved peak‑day policies, and waived fees.

How the Exchange Works

1. List or Request

Sellers list unused hours (subject to issuer transfer rules). Buyers request hours by cabin class, service area, and call‑out needs.

2. Verify & Lock

We KYC both sides, verify program status with the issuer, and lock terms in a standardized schedule. Buyer funds move to escrow.

3. Fly or Settle

Hours are applied to trips as you fly, or settled pro‑rata at the agreed rate. Unused balances may be re‑listed subject to terms.

Jet Cards vs Fractional Ownership

DimensionJet Card (prepaid access)Fractional Ownership (equity share)
What you buy Blocks of hours or a deposit with guaranteed or capped hourly rates and availability windows. Equity in a specific fleet program (e.g., 1/16–1/4 share) that converts to a contracted number of annual hours.
Up‑front outlay Typically low‑ to mid‑six figures depending on cabin and hours. High six to multi‑million depending on share size and cabin class, plus closing costs.
Ongoing fees Hourly rate + surcharges (fuel/CPI, de‑icing, peak). No monthly management fee. Monthly management fee + occupied hourly rate; owner responsible for share of maintenance/crew via program.
Term Months to a couple of years; some cards have expiry dates. 3–5+ years typical with defined remarketing/buy‑back terms.
Accounting Prepaid flight credit is usually a current asset (prepaid expense), expensed as flown. Capital asset on the balance sheet; potential residual value risk (and remarketing fees) at exit.
Best for 25–75 hours/year, flexibility across cabin classes, minimal admin. 80–200+ hours/year with predictable patterns and willingness to commit capital for guaranteed access.

We support both individuals and corporations. Program design, tax treatment and controls (e.g., department codes, traveler policies) can be tailored in onboarding.

Buyer Risks & FST Protections

Counterparty risk

Risk: Issuer financial stress or operational downtime can impair service or refunds.
FST: Escrow, multi‑issuer backup options, and a continuity rider that lets you elect a substitute provider at a pre‑agreed rate if service is unavailable.

Blackouts & call‑out limits

Risk: Holiday blackouts and long call‑outs reduce utility.
FST: Side‑by‑side disclosure of peak calendars and guaranteed call‑outs; optional premium to secure shorter call‑outs.

Surcharges & “gotchas”

Risk: Fuel/CPI escalators, de‑icing, taxi time minimums, FBO change fees.
FST: Unified rate card that computes true all‑in hourly and flags variances before you buy.

Transfer restrictions

Risk: Many T&Cs limit assignment/resale.
FST: Only list hours that issuers confirm as transferable; where not, we use issuer‑approved novation or “use-on-behalf” structures.
